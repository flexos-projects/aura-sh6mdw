# Aura

## 1. Vision

Every morning, millions of people stare into closets overflowing with clothes and feel a familiar, sinking dread: *I have nothing to wear.* 

The fashion industry has spent the last decade optimizing for the wrong thing. Fast fashion brands push micro-trends. E-commerce algorithms suggest items based on what you clicked, not what actually looks good on you. The result is a fragmented, chaotic wardrobe full of pieces that looked amazing on a 5'10" model under studio lighting, but wash you out and fit poorly in real life. 

Aura is the antidote to the endless, exhausting scroll of modern e-commerce. It is a wardrobe engine built on a simple, radical premise: great style is not about buying more clothes; it is about understanding your personal geometry.

Before Aura suggests a single garment, it performs a deep visual analysis of your biological reality. It maps your skin undertones, hair contrast, and eye color to determine your precise seasonal color palette. It analyzes your proportions, bone structure, and vertical line to map your architectural body type (inspired by the Kibbe system). 

Once Aura knows your rules, it parses the landscape of 2026 fashion—the silhouettes, the trending fabrics, the current palettes—and rigorously filters them. It doesn't just show you "what's trending." It shows you how to wear what's trending *for your specific architecture*. 

The people who use Aura are tired of buying clothes they only wear once. They want a capsule wardrobe of 30 pieces that mix and match into 100 perfect outfits. They want to walk into a room knowing their clothes are doing the work for them. They want the confidence of having a personal stylist, scaled through AI.

Success looks like a user scanning themselves, experiencing a profound "aha!" moment when they realize why that yellow sweater always made them look sick, and seamlessly buying a 5-piece capsule that transforms their daily routine from a source of stress into a moment of power.

Aura is not a shopping app. It is a mirror that finally shows you how good you can look.

## 2. Features

### The Biometric Style Scan
This is the onboarding experience that changes everything. Using the device camera, Aura captures a standardized set of images. The AI analyzes skin undertones (warm, cool, olive, neutral), high/low contrast levels between features, and bone structure (width, curve, vertical line). It outputs your definitive Style DNA: e.g., "True Winter with a Dramatic Classic geometry." The app immediately explains *why* certain things have never worked for you, building instant, unshakeable trust.

### The Dynamic Capsule Generator
Aura doesn't suggest individual, disconnected items. It suggests Capsules. You define a need ("I need a smart-casual office rotation for Spring") and Aura generates a 10-piece collection (3 bottoms, 4 tops, 2 shoes, 1 jacket) that mathematically interlock. It visualizes exactly how these 10 pieces combine into 24 distinct outfits, proving the ROI before you buy a single thing.

### Wardrobe Digitization & Integration
To build the perfect wardrobe, Aura needs to know what you already own. You can scan clothing tags or upload photos, and Aura instantly digitizes your existing closet. From that moment on, every new recommendation is cross-referenced. Aura will say: "This structured terracotta blazer works perfectly with your geometry, and it completes outfits with 4 items you already own."

### Trend Translation Engine
Fashion in 2026 moves fast. Aura tracks global runway and street-style trends, but it acts as a filter. If "oversized drop-shoulder knits" are trending, Aura knows that won't work for a "Romantic" body type. Instead, it translates the trend: "Get the cozy knit look, but opt for a cropped, waist-defining cardigan to honor your proportions."

### The Daily Stylist
Every morning, Aura checks the local weather and your calendar (syncs with Apple/Google). It presents three perfect outfit options built entirely from the clothes currently clean and available in your digitized wardrobe. It takes the cognitive load of getting dressed down to zero.

### Precision Fit Checkout
Because Aura knows your body geometry, it bypasses standard S/M/L sizing chaos. It integrates with multi-brand e-commerce APIs to find the exact size and cut that will drape correctly on your frame. It acts as a universal shopping cart, allowing you to buy a curated capsule from five different brands in one tap, with total confidence in the fit.

## 3. Pages

The experience begins before the user ever logs in. The landing page is an immersive, editorial scroll that explains the mathematics of style. It shows a slider: on the left, an outfit fighting a model's natural geometry; on the right, an outfit harmonizing with it. The call to action isn't "Shop Now," it's "Discover Your Architecture."

Upon signing up, the user enters The Scan. This is a guided, premium flow. The screen brightens to act as a ring light. The camera guides their face into an oval. It asks them to stand back for a full-body silhouette capture. A loading screen—designed to look like complex photogrammetry and color matrix calculations—builds anticipation. Then, The Reveal: a beautifully typeset dossier of their Style DNA. 

From there, they land on the Daily Dashboard. If it's morning, the hero section displays "Today's Look," offering three weather-appropriate outfits from their digital closet. Below this is the "Capsule Recommendations" feed—not an endless grid of random clothes, but curated, editorial horizontal scrolls of 5-10 items that form a complete concept (e.g., "The Tokyo Autumn Trip" or "Boardroom Essentials").

Tapping any recommended item opens the Item Detail page. This isn't a standard e-commerce page. The image is large, but the text below it is deeply personal. It explains exactly *why* this item works: "The structured shoulder honors your dramatic vertical line, while the deep emerald satisfies your Winter color contrast." Below this, a visual matrix shows the item paired with three things the user already owns.

The Digital Closet page is a visual inventory. Items are cleanly categorized by type, color, and season. Users can tap a "Generate Outfits" button to let the AI spin up new combinations from old clothes they've forgotten how to wear.

The Profile & Settings page holds their permanent Style DNA dossier, which they can reference while shopping in real life. It also contains their sizing data, brand exclusions (e.g., "I don't wear wool"), and privacy controls for their biometric data.

## 4. Data

The core of Aura's intelligence lies in how it connects abstract fashion concepts to hard biological rules. 

A `User` is not just an email and a password. They have a `StyleProfile` which contains their `ColorSeason` (e.g., Deep Autumn, Clear Spring) and their `BodyGeometry` (e.g., Flamboyant Gamine, Soft Classic). These profiles dictate the rules of the engine.

The inventory consists of `Garments`. A garment is heavily tagged data. It isn't just "A Red Sweater." It is "Color Hex: #8B0000, Temperature: Cool, Silhouette: Oversized, Fabric Drape: Heavy, Neckline: Crew." This level of metadata is what allows the AI to make precise matches.

When garments are combined, they form `Outfits`. An outfit has a specific occasion tag, weather rating, and a "Harmony Score" calculating how well the pieces work together based on color theory and proportion rules. 

A group of interlocking garments forms a `Capsule`. A capsule is a collection of 10-15 items that share a unified color story and silhouette logic, designed to generate maximum `Outfits` with minimal pieces. 

When a user purchases or uploads a garment, it enters their `Wardrobe`. The wardrobe tracks ownership, but also `WearHistory`—how often the item is selected in the Daily Stylist flow, allowing the app to calculate cost-per-wear and refine future recommendations based on actual behavior, not just aspiration.

## 5. Flows

### The Revelation (Onboarding)
Elena has always struggled with shopping. She downloads Aura. The app asks for camera permissions and guides her into a well-lit space. The screen flashes white to calibrate white balance, then takes a face scan and a full-body silhouette photo. 

A loading screen processes the data: *Analyzing skin undertone... Mapping facial contrast... Calculating vertical line...* 

The screen transitions to a beautiful editorial dossier. "Elena, you are a True Winter. Your body geometry is The Dramatic." It explains that her high-contrast coloring requires deep, icy jewel tones, and her sharp bone structure requires tailored, structured silhouettes. It shows her a visual gallery of her past fashion mistakes—slouchy beige sweaters—and explains *exactly* why they washed her out. She feels incredibly seen and understood. The confusion is gone. She taps "Build My First Capsule."

### The Morning Routine
It's 7:15 AM on a rainy Tuesday. Elena opens Aura. The app greets her: "Good morning. 52° and raining. You have a Board Meeting at 10 AM." 

Below, it presents "Today's Look": A charcoal structured blazer, a deep emerald silk camisole, tailored black trousers, and pointed leather boots—all items she already owns, digitized in her Wardrobe. It shows how the outfit perfectly hits her True Winter palette and honors her Dramatic vertical line. She taps "Wear This." The app logs the wear-count for those items, helping calculate her cost-per-wear over time. She gets dressed in four minutes and leaves the house feeling invincible.

### The Smart Purchase
Elena wants a new coat for the upcoming 2026 winter season. She opens Aura and searches "Winter Coats." Instead of thousands of results, she sees exactly four coats. 

She taps a structured, double-breasted navy overcoat. The page doesn't just show the price. It says: "Why this works: The sharp lapels honor your Dramatic bone structure, and Navy is a core neutral in your True Winter palette." Below that, a section reads "How to wear it." It displays three complete outfits, mixing the new coat with sweaters and trousers already in her digital closet. The app has proven the coat's utility before she buys it. She taps "Add to Cart," and Aura automatically selects a Size 6, knowing her exact measurements.

## 6. Design

The visual language of Aura is "Precision Editorial." It must not look like a traditional e-commerce app (Amazon, Zara) with endless, cluttered grids of products screaming for attention. It must look like a high-end fashion magazine that has been taken over by a brilliant mathematician. 

The UI is incredibly quiet. The background colors are deep, sophisticated neutrals—never pure black or pure white, but rich charcoals and soft alabasters. The typography does the heavy lifting: a sharp, elegant serif for display and editorial moments, paired with a hyper-modern geometric sans-serif for UI and a monospace font for measurements, hex codes, and data points. 

When a garment is displayed, it is treated like an artifact in a museum. Generous, deliberate white space surrounds it. The UI chrome is almost invisible. Borders are removed in favor of subtle elevation and shadow. 

The primary accent color—used for active states, primary buttons, and moments of delight—is a sophisticated, warm terracotta (`oklch(60% 0.15 45)`). It represents human warmth against the cold precision of the AI.

Motion is deliberate and unhurried. Elements don't snap into place; they glide with an elegant, slow ease. Hover states reveal metadata (fabric composition, color temperature) smoothly. The app feels expensive, calm, and deeply intelligent.

### Component Patterns & Microcopy

**The Garment Card:** Not a standard e-commerce box. It looks like a polaroid or an editorial clipping. An image with a subtle `--shadow-sm`. Below it, the designer name in `--font-mono` (xs), the item name in `--font-display` (lg), and a tiny tag indicating its match score (e.g., "98% Match"). No visible borders.

**The Matrix Matrix:** A visual grid showing one new item connected by thin lines to 3-4 owned items, proving the outfit combinations visually. 

**Microcopy Examples:**
- *Instead of "Shop Now":* "Analyze Capsule"
- *Instead of "Added to Cart":* "Reserved for your Wardrobe"
- *Instead of "No items found":* "We reject these trends for your geometry. Your perfect fit is elsewhere."
- *Instead of "Size Guide":* "Your Geometric Fit: Size 6"

## 9. Downstream Alerts

- ALERT: [Computer Vision Limitations] → Accurately determining skin undertone from a smartphone camera is notoriously difficult due to varying ambient lighting (warm bathroom lights vs. cool window light). We need a robust "calibration" step in the UI, perhaps asking the user to hold a piece of pure white paper in frame.
- ALERT: [E-commerce Integration] → The "Smart Cart" feature requires deep API integration with multi-brand retailers (like SSENSE, Farfetch, or Shopify aggregators). For v1, we may need to use affiliate linking rather than a native universal checkout.
- ALERT: [Vocabulary] → The terms "Kibbe" and "Color Season" have existing, vocal communities. We need to decide whether to use their exact terminology (which brings built-in SEO but potential purist backlash) or invent our own proprietary naming conventions (e.g., "Architectural Geometry"). → conversation agenda
